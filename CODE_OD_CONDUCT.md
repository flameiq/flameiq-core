# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

FlameIQ is a global open-source project. Contributors come from different countries, cultures, companies, and career stages. We are building infrastructure that engineers everywhere can trust — and that requires a community that everyone can participate in without fear of harassment, discrimination, or disrespect.

We commit to maintaining an environment where every contributor is treated with dignity and where participation is determined entirely by the quality of ideas and work, not by who someone is.

---

## Our Standards

### Expected behaviour

We expect all participants in the FlameIQ community to:

- **Be respectful and professional.** Treat every person in this community — regardless of experience level, background, or opinion — with the same respect you would expect in a professional engineering environment.
- **Assume good intent.** Most misunderstandings arise from miscommunication, not malice. When something feels off, start by assuming the other person meant well and ask a clarifying question before reacting.
- **Give and receive feedback constructively.** Code review is not personal. Critique the work, not the person. Accept critique on your own work with openness.
- **Be precise and honest.** In technical discussions, say what you mean. Acknowledge uncertainty when you are uncertain. Do not overstate confidence in positions you are not sure about.
- **Respect the RFC and review process.** Major decisions are made through documented, community-visible processes. If you disagree with a decision, engage through the proper channels — open a discussion, propose an RFC, or appeal to the maintainer team.
- **Welcome newcomers.** Being a senior engineer does not entitle you to be unkind to someone learning. Good first issues exist because we want new contributors to succeed.
- **Stay on topic.** FlameIQ's community spaces — issues, discussions, PRs — are for the project. Keep conversations relevant.

### Unacceptable behaviour

The following will not be tolerated under any circumstances:

- Harassment, intimidation, or threatening behaviour of any kind, public or private
- Discriminatory language or conduct based on gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, race, ethnicity, religion, nationality, disability, age, or any other personal characteristic
- Personal attacks, insults, or mockery directed at individuals
- Sustained disruption of discussions, reviews, or community spaces
- Publishing private information about another person without their explicit consent (doxxing)
- Sexualised language, imagery, or unwanted sexual attention in any community space
- Deliberately misrepresenting another person's position to attack a strawman
- Retaliating against someone who has reported a conduct concern

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all FlameIQ community spaces:

- GitHub Issues, Pull Requests, and Discussions
- The FlameIQ repository and all related repositories under the `flameiq` GitHub organisation
- Any official community communication channels (Slack, Discord, mailing lists, if established)
- FlameIQ representation at events, conferences, or meetups
- Direct messages between community members when those communications relate to the project

It applies to all participants: contributors, maintainers, new community members, and anyone interacting with the project in any capacity.

---

## Reporting a Concern

If you experience or witness behaviour that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it.

**How to report:**

Send an email to **[conduct@flameiq.dev](mailto:conduct@flameiq.dev)**

Your report will be received by the FlameIQ maintainer team. Include:

- A description of what happened
- Links or screenshots if available and relevant
- The approximate date and location (issue number, discussion link, etc.)
- Whether you want to be kept informed of the outcome

**Confidentiality:** All reports are treated as confidential. We will not reveal your identity to the person(s) involved without your explicit permission, except where required by law.

**Anonymous reports:** You may report anonymously if you prefer. Anonymous reports may limit our ability to follow up, but they are taken seriously and will be reviewed.

---

## Enforcement Process

The FlameIQ maintainer team will:

1. Acknowledge receipt of the report within **48 hours**
2. Review the report and gather context if needed
3. Reach a decision within **7 days** for most cases
4. Communicate the outcome to the reporter

### Possible outcomes

Depending on the severity and context of the violation, the maintainer team may take one or more of the following actions:

| Severity | Action |
|---|---|
| Minor, first occurrence | Private message acknowledging the issue and requesting a change in behaviour |
| Repeated minor or single moderate | Formal written warning with specific behavioural expectations |
| Severe or repeated after warning | Temporary suspension from community spaces |
| Most severe or sustained after suspension | Permanent ban from all FlameIQ community spaces |

Outcomes are determined on a case-by-case basis. Context matters. We do not apply mechanical enforcement.

### Appeals

If you believe an enforcement action was taken in error, you may appeal by emailing **[conduct@flameiq.dev](mailto:conduct@flameiq.dev)** with the subject line `[Appeal]`. A different member of the maintainer team will review the appeal and respond within 7 days.

---

## Responsibilities of Maintainers

Project maintainers are responsible for setting and upholding the standards in this document. Maintainers are held to the same standards as all other contributors — in fact, to a higher standard, given their visible role in the community.

Maintainers who violate this Code of Conduct may face consequences up to and including removal from the maintainer team, determined by the remaining maintainers.
